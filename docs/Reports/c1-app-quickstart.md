# BurnRate App Quick Start Guide

> TL;DR: open the comparison page, choose a scenario, scan the timeline and outcome summary, then use the sidebar sliders to stress the assumptions.

---

## Getting Started

- Open the BurnRate scenario comparison page.
- Pick one of the three scenario cards to open the full-year projection.
- Keep the comparison page and one or two scenario detail pages open in separate tabs if you want side-by-side review.

## The Three Scenarios

1. **Baseline**: no trust restructuring. Person A continues supporting C3 in the Legacy Condo unsustainably, available liquidity is used directly, and the household remains exposed to later care-cost drawdown.
2. **No Trust Buffer**: liquidity and leverage are used to create more short-term flexibility, but the assets still remain exposed outside the protection trust structure.
3. **Asset Protection**: the trust-based path, where MAPT and SNT structure, timing, and optimizer behavior work together to protect stability under high care costs.

## Detail View

- The detail page shows the full projection year by year.
- The top summary highlights solvency, depletion risk, and major planning outcomes.
- The sticky timeline and annotations show when care transitions, trust actions, and withdrawals activate.

## Sidebar Controls

- Use the parameter sidebar on the right to change assumptions.
- Important controls include:
  - LTC trigger year
  - memory care start year
  - year of passing
  - condo value
  - mortgage amount and split
  - optimizer enabled

## How To Read Results

- Focus first on depletion year, real-estate liquidation risk, and final trust balances.
- Then compare how the same change affects Baseline, Rental Buffer, and Asset Protection.
- Use the debug sidebar for per-year mechanics when something looks surprising.

## Current Demo Read

- In the current genericized demo state, **Baseline** and **No Trust Buffer** both end in visible depletion risk.
- **Asset Protection** is the only path that preserves a protected-trust outcome through the end of the projection.

## Practical Tip

- Change one variable at a time.
- If multiple outcomes move at once, return the slider to baseline and isolate the next assumption.
