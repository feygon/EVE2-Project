# MAPT Benefits and Responsibilities

**Prepared for Demo Review**

---

## Situation: Why Recovery Pressure Matters

Medicaid Estate Recovery is not passive. If the state has a valid claim after death, it can pursue reimbursement with persistence against assets that remain exposed, especially property that passes through the estate. In practice, that means families should not assume the state will simply overlook recoverable value or stop after an initial notice.

The MAPT matters because it is designed to reduce that exposure structurally. The goal is not to "hide" assets. The goal is to move the primary house into a properly administered trust early enough, and keep it outside the probate estate, so the house is less available as an easy recovery target later.

## What the MAPT Does

A Medicaid Asset Protection Trust moves major assets, especially the primary house, outside the grantor's direct ownership so they are better positioned against spend-down pressure and post-death recovery.

## Main Benefits

- Protects the primary house from being treated like ordinary spend-down property once the lookback is satisfied.
- Keeps the primary house outside probate, which is the core shield against estate-recovery exposure.
- Preserves the stepped-up basis strategy when paired with a properly drafted limited power of appointment.
- Gives the fiduciary a dedicated structure for housing-related cash management and documentation.

## Main Constraints

- The trust must be formed early enough for the lookback period to clear before Medicaid application.
- Assets placed into the trust are not casually reversible.
- Separate tax and legal structure means distributions and reimbursements must follow the trust terms, not household convenience.

## Trustee Responsibilities

- maintain the primary house
- pay taxes, insurance, and required upkeep on time
- keep trust funds separate from personal funds
- preserve records for every material trust transaction
- coordinate with counsel before any unusual distribution or restructuring step

## Demo Summary

The MAPT is the primary house-and-estate shield. Its value is structural: timing, probate separation, and disciplined fiduciary control.
